Abstract

This project studies handwritten Japanese character recognition on the KMNIST dataset. We compare multinomial logistic regression and several CNN architectures, with the tuned CNN achieving ~97% accuracy. Confusion matrices show most errors are visually ambiguous characters, highlighting the benefits of CNN representation learning and validation-based tuning.

Introduction

Handwritten character classification is a standard benchmark. KMNIST replaces MNIST digits with cursive Japanese Kuzushiji characters, making it challenging. We investigate logistic regression vs CNNs and the impact of hyperparameter tuning.

Methodology

We implemented Simple CNN, ResNet-like CNN, and an Improved CNN with batch normalization and dropout. Training and hyperparameter tuning details are included.

Results

ModelAccuracy

Research Extension: Few-Shot & Metric Learning

We evaluated a Simple CNN on K = 1,5,10 labeled samples per class and a Siamese network for one-shot classification. The Siamese model achieved 77.7% accuracy with only one support image per class.

Conclusion & Future Work

The tuned CNN achieved ~97% test accuracy. Future work includes data augmentation, deeper networks, and advanced regularization techniques.
